SFHTML5

web.dev: intentions, reflections, and aspirations

SFHTML5 is a developer group that hosts regular meetups in San Francisco. The event I attended in February was at Google, and they spoke about their new project web.dev. The objective of this project is to test your website on how well it supports its users. Example information includes opening on different device sizes, load time on a poor network connections, assistive technology, SEOs, performance, use of best practices, data security and more. Once you get your report, they provide assistance on how to improve and fix your site.

You can also explore the “learn” tab to discover all of the newest and best ways to build modern websites. It is broken down by collections and includes instructional videos. This was a great resource to discover and one that I know I will visit many times as I continue learning about front-end development. SFHTML5 is also be a great resource; they have monthly meetings to keep people up to date, and best of all, they record all of their meetings have have them available on YouTube! I will definitely be attending more of their events.
